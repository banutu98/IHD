What is this IHD?

IHD stands for Intracranial Hemorrhage Detection. Hemorrhage in the head (intracranial hemorrhage) is a relatively common condition that has many causes ranging from trauma, stroke, aneurysm, vascular malformations, high blood pressure, illicit drugs and blood clotting disorders. The role of the Radiologist is to detect the hemorrhage, characterize the hemorrhage subtype, its size and to determine if the hemorrhage might be jeopardizing critical areas of the brain that might require immediate surgery.

The system available in this web application is based on an deep neural network with an unique and complex structure. The neural network receives as an input a single dicom image of a brain or a sequence of dicom images from the same pacient and it predicts if that pacient has hemorrhage or not and if that is the case, which type of hemorrhage does he have. The system is successfully making predictions with an accuracy of 99.4 percent.
